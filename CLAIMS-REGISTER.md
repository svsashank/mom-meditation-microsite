# CLAIMS-REGISTER.md
> **This register must be read in full, not skimmed (BRIEF §10).** Every health- or benefit-related statement that appears on a research page is logged here with the strength of evidence behind it, the exact source, the practice actually studied, and whether that practice is the Miracle of Mind practice. A qualified medical/scientific reviewer must co-sign each row (fill `reviewer_signoff`) before any production launch (BRIEF §11).

**Status:** DRAFT — generated from auto-drafted page content. No rows are signed off yet. Claims are drawn verbatim from each study's own abstract conclusions and phrased with claim-strength language; they have not yet been checked against full text or human-edited.

**Totals:** 281 registered claims across 100 papers. 46 claims come from MoM-adjacent (breath-and-attention) practices; 235 from practices that are **not** the MoM practice (transfer gap noted on-page). 

**Includes a distinct claim class:** *mechanistic-transfer inference* rows — the "Why this matters for the 7-minute practice" bridges. Each states MoM shares a specific mechanism (breath regulation and/or directed attention) with the studied practice and that a benefit *may* extend to MoM, always ending "…though MoM hasn't been studied directly for this outcome." 72 papers carry one; 28 do not (no specific shared mechanism — see FLAGGED F24). These inferences need reviewer scrutiny of the *inference itself*, not just the source finding.

Machine-readable copy: `data/claims-register.csv`.

## How to read a row
- **Claim** — the statement as it appears (or is derived) on the page.
- **Strength** — evidence tier framing (meta-analytic / randomized-trial / longitudinal / mechanistic). Claims must never be phrased more strongly than this.
- **Practice / MoM?** — the practice actually studied, and whether it is the Miracle of Mind practice (Y), MoM-adjacent breath-and-attention (adjacent), or unrelated (N).
- **Source** — DOI of the single paper on the same page supporting the claim.
- **Sign-off** — blank until a qualified reviewer approves the claim and its phrasing.
